Reflection on Gender Roles and Relationships

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.'"

This quote made me pause. It speaks to a kind of love that isn’t really love at all, the kind that admires someone’s freedom, only to later try to take it away. I’ve seen this pattern in the world around me: people drawn to strong personalities, but uncomfortable when that strength shows up in ways they can’t control. Abel’s desire for a “traditional” wife was really about wanting dominance, not connection. And Patricia, who refused to be small, paid the price for being free.

What struck me is how relevant this still is. Even today, people praise “independent women” but expect them to shrink once they’re in a relationship. Being soft-spoken is expected, being assertive is often labeled as aggressive. There’s still a quiet pressure to make yourself more palatable. That’s why Patricia stands out, she refused to change who she was to make Abel feel more powerful. Her strength wasn’t loud or performative, it was steady. She raised Trevor to think for himself, to see beyond systems designed to make him feel less. And that made her dangerous in the eyes of a man who needed control to feel whole.

This quote resonates with ongoing conversations about gender roles in modern relationships. Many people still idealize the past, wanting the benefits of contemporary equality while craving control rooted in patriarchy. Abel is not unique, his mindset reflects how society romanticizes domination under the guise of protection or structure. In today’s world, freedom and equality in relationships must coexist with vulnerability and mutual respect. Women should not have to surrender their autonomy to be loved fully. True love doesn’t fear freedom; it makes room for it. Patricia understood that. Abel never did.

The Rebel’s Colorblind Son

If I were to reimagine the title of this book, it would be: The Rebel’s Colorblind Son. This reflects the core of Trevor Noah’s story, a boy born into a divided world, taught to see beyond its lines by a fiercely independent mother. Patricia's rebellion against the system shaped Trevor’s values, humor, and identity. This title honors their bond and the way he inherited her fighting spirit in a society obsessed with labels.
